# ASO and Competitor Analysis

> Superseded: current app name and first-launch metadata are now defined in [ASO_SideProfit_Metadata_2026-05-21.md](ASO_SideProfit_Metadata_2026-05-21.md).

Research date: 2026-05-12

Product: Indie Developer Finance Tracker

## Executive Summary

The app should not compete head-on with generic expense trackers. That category is crowded, broad, and difficult to rank in without paid acquisition.

The stronger positioning is:

> A profit and break-even tracker for indie developers building apps, SaaS, and side projects.

This creates a narrower but more defensible market:

- Indie developers who want to know whether an app is becoming profitable.
- Side project builders tracking revenue, expenses, time cost, and break-even progress.
- Build-in-public creators who want shareable progress cards.

The main ASO opportunity is to combine keywords from three markets:

- Finance: expense tracker, income tracker, profit tracker
- Business: revenue tracker, MRR, project income
- Developer/startup: indie developer, SaaS, side project, app revenue

## Market Categories

### 1. Generic Finance Trackers

These apps focus on personal budgets, income/expense tracking, subscriptions, and spending analytics.

Examples:

- Finsight: Expense Tracker
- Surplus: Expense Tracker
- Everplan: Expense Tracker
- Money Life: Expense Tracker

Strengths:

- Broad keyword coverage.
- Familiar mental model.
- Many support budgeting, categories, charts, and multi-currency.

Weaknesses:

- Not project-based.
- Not built for app/SaaS/product revenue.
- Usually no break-even model.
- Usually no time cost or developer-specific expense categories.
- Emotional framing is about saving money, not validating a project.

Implication:

Do not position as another personal finance app. Use finance keywords, but make the product promise project/business-specific.

### 2. Small Business / Side Hustle Profit Trackers

These apps focus on sales, expenses, inventory, gross profit, net profit, and exports.

Examples:

- Profit Tracker - Made Simple
- MyRevenue
- Vertex - Revenue Tracker

Strengths:

- Closer to the target use case than personal finance apps.
- Profit/loss language is already familiar.
- Some include multi-business or multi-project workflows.

Weaknesses:

- Often aimed at resellers, sellers, freelancers, or small businesses.
- Usually not designed around app developers.
- May focus on inventory, invoices, clients, or sales platforms instead of MRR, API cost, App Store fees, and build progress.

Implication:

This is the closest commercial category. The product should borrow "profit tracker" and "revenue tracker" language, then differentiate with "for indie developers", "break-even", "MRR", and "project ROI".

### 3. Indie Developer Revenue Trackers

These apps are closer emotionally and strategically, but most focus on connected revenue dashboards rather than manual project finance.

Examples:

- IndieBar - Revenue Tracker
- SoldIt: Revenue Tracker
- ADWize: AD Revenue Tracker

Strengths:

- Clear indie developer audience.
- Strong dopamine loop around revenue updates, MRR, sales, renewals, and milestones.
- Privacy is often part of the pitch.

Weaknesses:

- Usually focused on one or more integrations such as Stripe, RevenueCat, App Store Connect, GA4, AdMob, or AdSense.
- Not ideal for early-stage projects before revenue integrations exist.
- Often not mobile-first project planning tools.
- Less emphasis on total cost, hidden labor cost, and break-even progress.

Implication:

The app can enter earlier in the indie developer journey: before the project is big enough to justify connecting Stripe, RevenueCat, or App Store Connect.

## Positioning Recommendation

Primary positioning:

> Track whether your side project is becoming profitable.

Secondary positioning:

> Revenue, expenses, time cost, and break-even progress for indie developers.

Avoid leading with:

- Budget tracker
- Personal finance
- Accounting
- Bookkeeping

Use those only as supporting keywords.

## Differentiation

The product should own these concepts:

1. Break-even progress per project
2. Hidden time cost and real ROI
3. Developer-specific expense categories
4. Indie project dashboard
5. Emotional progress feedback
6. Build-in-public sharing cards
7. Multi-project tracking for app/SaaS builders

This is more distinct than "expense tracker with charts".

## Recommended App Names

### Best Option

Name:

> IndieProfit

Why:

- Short and memorable.
- Communicates the target user and outcome.
- Works for both iOS and Google Play 30-character title limits.
- Leaves room for keyword subtitle.

iOS:

- App Name: `IndieProfit`
- Subtitle: `App Revenue & Break-even`

Google Play:

- Title: `IndieProfit: Revenue Tracker`
- Short description: `Track app revenue, expenses, time cost, and break-even progress.`

### Other Good Options

| Name | Pros | Cons |
| --- | --- | --- |
| MakerLedger | Strong maker/finance feel | "Ledger" may feel too accounting-heavy |
| LaunchLedger | Good for side projects and launches | Less direct about profit |
| ProjectProfit | Very clear keyword name | More generic, weaker brand |
| ShipLedger | Nice if tied to ShipSwift/startup theme | Less obvious to non-developers |
| BreakEven | Very clear core feature | Too generic and may limit future scope |

### iOS Metadata

App Name:

`IndieProfit`

Subtitle:

`App Revenue & Break-even`

Promotional Text:

`See if your side project is actually moving forward. Track revenue, expenses, time cost, and break-even progress in one focused dashboard.`

Keyword Field Draft:

`indie,profit,revenue,tracker,break even,mrr,expense,startup,founder,project,saas,side hustle`

Note:

Apple's keyword field is limited to 100 characters and should use comma-separated terms without spaces after commas. Final keyword field should be tuned again after the exact app name and subtitle are locked.

### Google Play Metadata

Title:

`IndieProfit: Revenue Tracker`

Short Description:

`Track app revenue, expenses, time cost, and break-even progress.`

Long Description Draft:

`IndieProfit helps indie developers and side project builders understand whether their apps are actually becoming profitable.`

`Track project revenue, expenses, time cost, and break-even progress in one focused dashboard. Instead of guessing whether your side project is working, see your real numbers clearly: income, costs, net profit, hidden development cost, and how far you are from break-even.`

`Built for app developers, SaaS builders, solo founders, and makers managing multiple projects.`

Key features:

- Track income and expenses per project
- See net profit and loss
- Monitor break-even progress
- Track hidden development time cost
- Use developer-focused categories such as server, API, App Store fees, domains, AI tools, ads, and design
- View monthly revenue and expense trends
- Celebrate milestones like first revenue, first profitable month, and reaching break-even
- Generate shareable progress cards for build-in-public updates

`IndieProfit is not a personal budget app. It is a project profitability tracker for people building real products.`

## Screenshot Strategy

The first 3 screenshots matter most because they appear in search/product browsing contexts.

Recommended screenshot order:

1. `Is your app profitable yet?`
   - Show project dashboard with revenue, expenses, net profit, and break-even progress.

2. `See how close you are to break-even`
   - Show progress bar, remaining amount, and estimated timeline.

3. `Track the real cost of building`
   - Show hidden time cost and effective hourly return.

4. `Built for indie developers`
   - Show developer categories: API, server, domain, AI tools, App Store fee.

5. `Share your progress publicly`
   - Show build-in-public card template.

6. `Manage all your side projects`
   - Show project list with multiple apps/SaaS/products.

Tone:

- Clean, product-led, not cartoonish.
- Use real dashboard UI, not abstract marketing art.
- Keep screenshot text short.
- Avoid claims like "best", "#1", "free", or pricing text.

## MVP Impact From ASO

The ASO analysis changes the MVP priority.

Must-have for launch:

- Login and cloud sync
- Project-based income/expense tracking
- Break-even dashboard
- Time cost tracking
- Developer-specific expense categories
- Monthly trend chart
- Basic milestone detection
- At least 3 share card templates

Can wait:

- AI insights
- Revenue integrations
- Advanced forecasting
- Team collaboration
- Full offline conflict sync

Reason:

If the listing promises indie developer profitability, the first session must immediately answer:

> Is this project profitable, and how far is it from breaking even?

## Subscription Implications

Free plan should allow users to feel the core value quickly.

Recommended Free:

- 1 project
- 50 transactions
- Basic break-even dashboard
- Basic monthly chart
- 1 share template
- Cloud sync included

Recommended Pro:

- Unlimited projects
- Unlimited transactions
- Time cost tracking
- Advanced analytics
- Full milestone history
- Premium share templates
- CSV export
- Future revenue integrations

Recommended pricing:

- Monthly: US$4.99
- Yearly: US$39.99
- Taiwan localized reference: NT$150/month, NT$1,190/year

Important:

Cloud sync should not be Pro-only. For a finance tracker, cross-device backup is baseline trust, not a premium luxury.

## Launch Recommendation

Start with iOS first if ShipSwift already gives you auth, subscription, and app template speed.

Reason:

- Indie developers are reachable on X, Threads, Reddit, Product Hunt, and indie maker communities.
- App Store users are more accustomed to subscription productivity/business tools.
- iOS screenshots and paywall iteration can be done faster before expanding to Android.

After iOS MVP:

1. Validate positioning and screenshots.
2. Watch conversion from product page to install.
3. Watch activation: project created + first transaction added.
4. Watch paywall trigger: second project or time cost feature.
5. Then consider Android.
